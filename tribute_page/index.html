Annelies Marie „Anne“ Frank

Es ist ein Wunder, dass ich nicht alle Erwartungen aufgegeben habe, denn sie scheinen absurd und unausführbar. Trotzdem halte ich an ihnen fest, trotz allem, weil ich noch immer an das Gute im Menschen glaube.

Tagebuch, Samstag 15. Juli 1944

Frank pictured in 1940

Until today
The book has become a universal representation of the time which is widely used in education around the world.
1955,1959
In 1955, the diary was adapted as play in New York, being followed by a film adapation in 1959.
1947-1952
Otto Frank pursued the release of Anne's diary as a book but was unsuccessful until it got finally released in 1947 in the Netherlands, being followed by further publications in France, Germany as well as US and Japan. After that it soon became a world wide success.
Apr 1945
British troops freed the Auschwitz camp on April 15th. As Otto Frank got separated from his family on arrival in Auschwitz, he only learned later after release of the deaths of his wife and two daughters. He got Anne's diarys from a family friend after he learned of her death.
Feb/Mar 1945
Anne Frank died in February or March (exact date is unknown) in the KZ Auschwitz, most probably because of some illness. Her mother died of starvation, her sister was taken by illness as well. Only her father survived.
Aug 1944
On August 4th, the Achterhuis was stormed by Gestapo and SS, following a tip from an until now unidentified source. All three families were arrested and held as criminals before being deported to transition camps and further deportation to work camps. The Franks were deported with the last transportation train to Auschwitz late 1944.
1942-1944
Anne Frank frequently wrote in her diary. In the beginning about the restrictions and discrimination the Jewish family faced by the Dutch Nazis, later about the relationships within her family and with the friends that joined them in their hiding place.
1942
For her 13th birthday, Anne Frank got a notebook from her father, which she started to use as a diary. In July, the family went into hiding to prevent persecution and deportation into work camps. Their hiding place is known as Achterhuis. The hiding place was located above one of Otto's old companies, hidden by a bookcase. Only a handful employees of the company knew about the hidden family. They were joined by two befriended families by the end of the year.
1941
Otto Frank sold his two companies to prevent a forced closing by the government because of being Jewish led companies. A plan to flee to the US was unsuccessful as the US government refused to accept and help Nazi refugees.
1940
In May, Nazi Germany invaded the Netherlands and the persecution of Jews by restrictive and discriminatory laws, mandatory registration and segregation.
1934
The Franks fled Germany to Amsterdam, Netherlands, where Otto Frank was offered to start a company.
1929
On June 12th, Anneliese Marie Frank was born in Frankfurt, Germany, as daughter to Edith and Otto Heinrich Frank, a Jewish family.
